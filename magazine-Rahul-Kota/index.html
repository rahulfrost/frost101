Discovering the Benefits of Reading

Act of reading

The act of reading has been a revered and highly valued art form throughout human history. Its importance cannot be overstated, as it has been instrumental in shaping and propelling the development of human civilization.

Improving Perspective

As you read, your mind becomes detached from the present moment, enabling you to concentrate on the words in front of you. Reading can transform your outlook on the events happening around you, aiding you in developing into the individual you aspire to become.

memory

A study conducted at Stavanger University revealed that the brain creates a mental image of written text based on its location on the page and the position of individual words. To enhance cognitive activity and memory function, engaging in the tactile experience of reading a physical book is crucial.

Sleep & Relaxation

Engaging in the act of reading an old-fashioned novel can not only enhance brain function and memory but also promote better sleep. With the ever-increasing amount of time we spend in front of screens, it is crucial to disconnect and unwind, particularly before retiring to bed.

mental health

Reading helps us understand our surroundings and improves our ability to understand others' thoughts. Studies show that reading fiction increases activation in the prefrontal cortex, which is associated with perspective-taking in social contexts.

How to read

Adler sets forth his method for reading a non-fiction book in order to gain understanding. He claims that three distinct approaches, or readings, possible out of a book, but that performing these three levels of readings does not necessarily mean reading the book three times, as the experienced reader will be able to do all three in the course of reading the book just once. Adler names the readings "structural", "interpretative", and "critical", in that order.
